Lasagna

Description

Lasagna, a beloved Italian dish, is a comforting and satisfying meal that has captured hearts and taste buds worldwide. Its layers of rich, meaty sauce, tender pasta sheets, and creamy cheese create a symphony of flavors in every bite. The aroma of simmering sauce and baking cheese fills the kitchen, promising a culinary indulgence that is hard to resist. Whether enjoyed on a chilly evening or as a special occasion treat, lasagna is a dish that brings people together and warms the soul.

Beyond its classic Italian roots, lasagna has evolved into a versatile dish that can be adapted to suit various dietary preferences and tastes. From vegetarian and vegan versions to creative variations with seafood or different types of cheese, there's a lasagna to satisfy every palate. Its ability to be customized and shared makes it a crowd-pleasing favorite for gatherings and family dinners. Lasagna is more than just a meal; it is a culinary experience that brings joy and satisfaction to those who savor its deliciousness.

Ingredients

  • Olive oil
  • Onions
  • Celery
  • Carrots
  • Ground beef
  • Tomato paste
  • Red wine
  • Canned, peeled tomatoes
  • Butter
  • Milk
  • Flour
  • Nutmeg
  • Fresh lasagna pasta sheets
  • Mozzarella
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Basil
  • Salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Preheat your pan with the olive oil inside on a medium-high heat. Once heated add the onions, celery and carrots and cook until they start to get fragrant.
  2. Turn up the heat to high and add your ground beef. Cook until it starts to get a brown color.
  3. Add tomato paste and let it heat up. After that pour in your red wine and let it reduce.
  4. Then add your peeled tomatoes and fill up the pan with water. Let it cook on a low heat for at least two hours to get a nice flavour. Season with salt and pepper if needed.
  5. Preheat your oven to 200Â°C and melt some butter in another saucepan.
  6. Once melted add a tablespoon of flour. Then pour in the milk and let it cook until the sauce thickens. Season with pepper and some nutmeg.
  7. Start layering your lasagna in an oven form. Start with your Bolognese followed by the Bechamel sauce and the fresh lasagna sheets. Stack until you reach the moon (or run out of Bolognese).
  8. Add you Mozzarella and Parmesan cheese on top and put the lasagna into the oven for 30 min. Garnish with some fresh basil.
